SEN Policy

Arden Primary School SEN Policy The governing body along with the head teacher, is responsible for the provision of pupils with SEN and will report annually to parents of children with SEN. The SENCO will work closely with the head teacher and will have sole responsibility for the day to day operation of the school’s SEN policy Objectives of the policy

• All pupils at Arden Primary School will have access to a balanced curriculum.

• All staff are responsible and able to identify, assess, monitor and teach pupils with SEN.

• All pupils with SEN will be included in the educational and social life of the school.

• All pupils with SEN will be actively involved in their own learning.

• Arden Primary school works in partnership with the Local Authority and outside agencies to ensure there is an approach that is professional and aims to meet the needs of all learners.

SEN provision

The SENCO will:

• Work with colleagues, parents, pupils and outside agencies to design, monitor and review any objectives on Individual Education Plans

• Monitoring pupil achievement and use information to inform further planning

• Provide guidance and continuing professional development for all staff

• Liaise regularly with parents and carers of pupils with SEN

• Liaise with external agencies

• Take full responsibility for the management of the SEN teachers and teaching assistants

Inclusion of pupils with SEN

It is the responsibility of all teachers to include pupils with SEN when teaching in the classroom and to plan, manage and support pupils to enable them to access a balanced curriculum. In line with the Disability Discrimination Act 1995 (as amended by the SEN and Disability Act, 2001

Parents/Carers

The school will work in partnership with parents and carers to achieve aims:

• Parents and carers will have the opportunity engage in their child’s education

• Parents and carers will be encouraged to inform the school of any difficulties their child may be having.

• Instilling confidence that the school will listen and act appropriately

• Arden Primary School will Focus on the child’s strengths and difficulties

• Parents and carers will be given the opportunity to discuss ways in which they can help their child

• Parents will be included when agreeing targets for the child

• Parents and carers will be informed about any support during assessment and any decision-making process about SEN provision

Pupils Involvement

All pupils have the right to be involved in making decisions and choices. pupils will be made are aware of their individual rights and needs Steps will be taken to involve pupils in decisions which are taken regarding their education,
